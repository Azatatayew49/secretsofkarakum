4-Day Discovery of Turkmenistan

From Marble City to Sacred Mountains — From 710 USD per person

Start: Ashgabat Airport
End: Ashgabat Airport
Days: 4
Group size: 3+
Price: From 710 USD/pax

Discover the essence of Turkmenistan on this immersive 4-day journey through its striking capital, sacred mountain landscapes, desert wonders, and cultural heritage. From the white-marble grandeur of Ashgabat to the burning Darvaza Gas Crater and the preserved traditions of Nohur village, this itinerary offers a rich combination of history, nature, and authentic local character.

Itinerary

Day 1 — Arrival in Ashgabat: The White-Marble Capital

Arrival at Ashgabat International Airport, where you will be met by your guide and transferred to the city. Your journey begins with an introduction to Ashgabat, the capital of Turkmenistan, famous for its monumental architecture, broad avenues, immaculate urban planning, and white-marble facades. The first sightseeing visits include the Independence Monument and the Neutrality Monument. Continue to the Alem Cultural and Entertainment Center to see the famous indoor Ferris wheel. You will also visit the Memorial Complex. In the evening, enjoy a panoramic night tour of Ashgabat. Overnight at a hotel in Ashgabat.

Distance of the day: approx. 30–50 km

Day 2 — Ashgabat – Old Nisa – Akhal-Teke Horses – Ruhy Mosque – Darvaza

After breakfast, continue exploring the historical and cultural highlights of Ashgabat and its surroundings. Begin with a visit to the National Museum of Turkmenistan. Then proceed to Old Nisa, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most significant archaeological monuments in Central Asia — once a royal residence of the Parthian Empire. The tour continues with a visit to the Akhal-Teke horse stables, where you will see Turkmenistan's most celebrated national symbol, known for their elegance, intelligence, endurance, and distinctive metallic sheen. Next, visit the grand Turkmenbashi Ruhy Mosque in Gypjak. After lunch, depart Ashgabat and drive north through the Karakum Desert toward Darvaza. By evening, arrive at the famous Darvaza Gas Crater, often referred to as the “Door to Hell.” Enjoy a barbecue dinner near the crater. Overnight in tents/yurt-style desert camp near Darvaza.

Distance of the day: approx. 300 km

Day 3 — Darvaza – Ashgabat

Wake up early to experience the peaceful desert morning and the unique scenery of Darvaza in daylight. After breakfast at the camp, depart for the return drive to Ashgabat. Upon arrival in the capital, enjoy lunch at a local restaurant. In the afternoon, visit Lenin Park, one of Ashgabat's pleasant green spaces. Later, visit the Ertugrul Gazi Mosque, notable for its Ottoman-inspired design and refined architectural details. Overnight at a hotel in Ashgabat.

Distance of the day: approx. 280 km

Day 4 — Ashgabat – Nohur Village – Kow Ata Underground Lake – Departure

After breakfast, depart for the scenic mountain region southwest of Ashgabat. Drive into the Kopetdag foothills to visit Nohur, a remote mountain village known for its distinctive customs, traditional way of life, and beautiful natural surroundings. Continue to Kow Ata Underground Lake, a remarkable natural site hidden inside a cave, known for its warm mineral-rich water. Those who wish may enjoy a swim in its naturally heated waters. After lunch, return to Ashgabat and transfer to Ashgabat International Airport for departure. End of the tour.

Distance of the day: approx. 180–220 km
