7-Day Grand Expedition Across Turkmenistan

From Ancient Civilizations to Natural Wonders — From 1350 USD per person

Start: Ashgabat
End: Ashgabat
Days: 7
Group size: 3+
Price: From 1350 USD/pax

This 7-day expedition offers a deep and diverse journey through Turkmenistan’s most remarkable cultural, historical, and natural highlights. From the white-marble grandeur of Ashgabat to the flaming Darvaza Gas Crater, from the ancient Silk Road legacy of Merv to the dramatic landscapes of Yangykala Canyons and the traditions of mountain villages, this itinerary combines archaeology, spirituality, desert adventure, and scenic beauty into one unforgettable experience.

Itinerary

Day 1 — Arrival in Ashgabat: The White-Marble Capital

Arrival at Ashgabat International Airport, where you will be met by your guide and transferred to your hotel. Your journey begins in Ashgabat, renowned for its monumental architecture, elegant urban planning, and striking white-marble buildings. Start your city discovery with visits to the Independence Monument and the Neutrality Monument. Continue to the Alem Cultural and Entertainment Center to see the famous indoor Ferris wheel. You will also visit the Memorial Complex. In the evening, enjoy a panoramic night tour of Ashgabat. Overnight at a hotel in Ashgabat.

Distance of the day: approx. 30–50 km

Day 2 — Ashgabat – Old Nisa – Akhal-Teke Horses – Ruhy Mosque – Darvaza

After breakfast, continue exploring the cultural and historical highlights of Ashgabat and its surroundings. Begin with a visit to the National Museum of Turkmenistan. Then continue to Old Nisa, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most significant archaeological monuments in Central Asia — once a royal residence of the Parthian Empire. The tour continues with a visit to the Akhal-Teke horse stables — known for their elegance, endurance, and distinctive metallic sheen. Next, visit the grand Turkmenbashi Ruhy Mosque in Gypjak. After lunch, depart Ashgabat and drive north through the Karakum Desert toward Darvaza. By evening, arrive at the world-famous Darvaza Gas Crater. Enjoy a barbecue dinner near the crater. Overnight in tents or yurt-style desert camp near Darvaza.

Distance of the day: approx. 300 km

Day 3 — Darvaza – Ashgabat

Wake up to the peaceful atmosphere of the Karakum Desert and enjoy the unique morning scenery around the crater. After breakfast at the camp, depart for the return drive to Ashgabat. Upon arrival, enjoy lunch at a local restaurant. Spend some time at Lenin Park. Later, visit the elegant Ertugrul Gazi Mosque, notable for its Ottoman-inspired design. Overnight at a hotel in Ashgabat.

Distance of the day: approx. 280 km

Day 4 — Ashgabat – Mary – Ancient Merv – Ashgabat

After an early breakfast, depart for the historic region of Mary. Today’s highlight is the archaeological park of Ancient Merv, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the oldest and most important oasis cities of Central Asia. During the excursion, explore the Great Kyz Kala and Little Kyz Kala, the Mausoleum of Sultan Sanjar, and the Mausoleum of Yusuf Hamadani. After the excursion, return to Ashgabat. Overnight at a hotel in Ashgabat.

Distance of the day: approx. 700–740 km

Day 5 — Ashgabat – Turkmenbashi – Yangykala Canyons – Serdar

In the morning, transfer to the airport and take a domestic flight from Ashgabat to Turkmenbashi, opening a new chapter of the journey in western Turkmenistan. Upon arrival, drive toward the spectacular Yangykala Canyons — often described as the “Grand Canyon of Central Asia,” famous for its dramatic limestone formations and vivid bands of red, pink, orange, and white. Spend time enjoying the panoramic viewpoints before continuing to Serdar for the evening. Overnight at a hotel in Serdar.

Distance of the day: approx. 320–380 km by road, plus domestic flight

Day 6 — Serdar – Nohur Village – Kow Ata Underground Lake – Ashgabat

After breakfast, continue your journey toward the Kopetdag mountain region. Visit Nohur, a distinctive mountain village known for its preserved traditions, unique local customs, and scenic mountain surroundings. Later, continue to Kow Ata Underground Lake, hidden inside a cave and known for its warm, mineral-rich waters. Those who wish may enjoy a swim before the final drive back to the capital. Arrive in Ashgabat in the evening. Overnight at a hotel in Ashgabat.

Distance of the day: approx. 500–560 km

Day 7 — Ashgabat Bazaar Visit – Departure

After breakfast, enjoy a visit to one of Ashgabat’s local bazaars, where you can experience a more authentic and everyday side of the city. Depending on your departure time, you may also enjoy some free time for rest or independent exploration before your transfer to Ashgabat International Airport. End of the tour.

Distance of the day: approx. 20–40 km
