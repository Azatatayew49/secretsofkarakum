Highlights of Turkmenistan

Discover the most remarkable treasures of a hidden gem

Main Highlights

Discover the most remarkable treasures of Turkmenistan — from ancient civilizations to breathtaking natural wonders. Each destination tells a unique story of history, culture, and nature.

Ashgabat – The White Marble City

Explore the extraordinary capital of Turkmenistan, famous for its grand white marble architecture, wide avenues, monuments, and unique modern cityscape. A city unlike any other in Central Asia.

UNESCO World Heritage Site

Old Nisa

Visit the ancient Parthian fortress of Old Nisa, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most important historical landmarks of the region. Once the royal residence of the Parthian Empire.

Gas Crater “Door to Hell”

Witness the world-famous burning gas crater in the heart of the Karakum Desert — one of the most iconic and unforgettable sights in Turkmenistan. Especially spectacular at night.

UNESCO World Heritage Site

Ancient Merv

Discover the ruins of Ancient Merv, one of the great oasis cities of the Silk Road and a UNESCO World Heritage Site rich in history, culture, and archaeological significance.

Yangykala Canyons

Admire the stunning landscapes of the Yangykala Canyons, with their colorful cliffs and dramatic rock formations creating one of the most spectacular natural views in Turkmenistan — often called the “Grand Canyon of Central Asia.”

Akhal-Teke Horses

Meet the legendary Akhal-Teke horses, one of the oldest and most beautiful horse breeds in the world, considered a true symbol of Turkmen heritage. Known for their distinctive metallic sheen and extraordinary grace.

UNESCO World Heritage Site

Kunya-Urgench

Explore Kunya-Urgench, another UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its impressive medieval monuments and deep spiritual significance as the ancient capital of Khorezm.

Underground Lake “Kow Ata”

Experience the unique underground lake of Kow Ata, a natural thermal site hidden inside a cave, known for its warm mineral water and unusual atmosphere. Visitors can swim in its naturally heated waters.
